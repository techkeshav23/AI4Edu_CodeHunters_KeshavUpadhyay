# Rulebook: Pre-AI Summit Hackathon on AI for Social Good

**Event:** Pre-AI Impact Summit 2026 Hackathon (24 Hours)
**Organizers:** School of AI & Data Engineering (AIDE), IIT Ropar
**Hosting Partner:** Advitiya (IIT Ropar's Tech Fest)
**Technical Sponsors:** NxtGen and C-DAC

---

## 1. Purpose & Overview
This 24-hour pan-India hackathon is one of the Pre-AI Summit Activities at IIT Ropar being organized before the **India AI Impact Summit 2026**. The goal is to expose student teams to real-world AI challenges and accelerate the development of prototype solutions under mentorship from faculty and domain experts.

**Expected outputs include:**
* Working prototype/MVP (acceptable)
* Reproducible code and benchmarking
* Open-source-ready artifacts (where feasible)

---

## 2. Themes / Tracks
The hackathon will include 3 tracks aligned to societal/industry needs:
1. **AI for Healthcare** (Note: "AI for Agriculture" is mentioned in Registration details, likely an alias or specific focus)
2. **AI for Education**
3. **AI for Low-Light Object Detection**

---

## 3. On-site Release Policy
To ensure fairness and prevent pre-training advantage:
* The **exact problem statements and datasets** will be shared only at IIT Ropar, **1 hour before the hackathon begins**, i.e., at **3 PM on 6 Feb 2026**.
* Teams must not attempt to obtain dataset links or problem details before the official release.
* Any violation may result in disqualification.

---

## 4. Eligibility
* **Target Audience:** UG/PG/PhD Indian students graduating in **2027, 2028, and 2029**.

---

## 5. Team Size, Registration & Confirmation Rules
* **Team Size:** 4 members.
* Each participant can be part of only one team.
* **Registration:** Open on Unstop till **23:59 on 30 Jan 2026 (Friday)**.
* During registration, teams must select one of three themes (AI for Agriculture, AI for Education, or AI for Low-light object detection) and upload CVs of all 4 members.
* **Selection:** Teams will be selected based on participants' CVs. Discrepancies or false info will lead to disqualification. IIT Ropar reserves the right to make the final decision.
* **Notification:** Selected teams will be notified by **31 Jan 2026 (Saturday)**.
* **Confirmation:** Selected teams must confirm participation by **16:59 on 1 Feb 2026 (Sunday)** by:
    1. Confirming participation.
    2. Paying **₹700** for food during the event.
    3. Informing accommodation choices.
    * *Failure to confirm will result in the slot being forfeited and offered to the waiting list.*

---

## 6. General Rules
1. All development must start only **after** the hackathon begins.
2. Projects must be submitted before the deadline; late submissions will not be evaluated.
3. **Pre-written code is not allowed**, except:
    * Open-source libraries
    * Framework boilerplates
    * Public APIs
4. Continuous development during the event is expected.
5. **Plagiarism/copying existing projects** results in immediate disqualification.
6. Submissions must be the original work of the team.
7. The project must align with the announced theme/problem statement.
8. Solutions should be functional (prototype/MVP acceptable).
9. The idea must be clearly explained and demonstrable.
10. Judges' decisions are final and binding.
11. IP ownership rules must be respected as stated by organizers during release (Default: team-owned unless stated otherwise).

---

## 9. Stay and Accommodation
Teams can choose one of the following:
1.  **Non-stay option (Free):** No accommodation provided. Use venue toilets/hostel showers and store luggage in a common room.
2.  **Stay:** Hostel dorm accommodation for **₹250 per person per day**. Mattress and blanket provided.

## 10. Submission Requirements
Each team must submit:
1.  **Public GitHub repository** containing:
    * Environment setup (`requirements.txt` or `environment.yml`)
    * Source code
    * Instructions for training/inference
    * Scripts to generate final outputs
2.  **Output files** in the exact format specified at release time.
3.  **Submission Form:** Filled out through the official form shared on-site.
* **Limit:** Max 2 final submissions per team. Late submissions are not evaluated.

---

## 11. Judging & Evaluation
Evaluation details announced on-site. Broad criteria:
* Primary metric performance
* Reproducibility and code quality
* Experimental rigor
* Practicality/efficiency
* Bonus: Demo/prototype readiness
* *Checkpoint evaluations will be used to monitor progress.*

---

## 12. Track Notes

### Track 1: AI for Agriculture
* **Focus Areas:** Multi-view learning and Growth Modeling, Regression-style prediction, Compute-aware learning (limited views/budget), Robust evaluation.

### Track 2: AI for Education
* **Preparation:** Teams are expected to explore **rPPG extraction methods** beforehand.
* **Algorithms:** Teams may use any 3 of these 7 algorithms: *CHROM, POS, DeepPhys, PhysNet, EfficientPhys, PhysFormer, TS-CAN*.

### Track 3: AI for Low-Light Object Detection
* **Focus Areas:** Underwater/Fog/Challenging Vision, Image enhancement/restoration, Detection/tracking under low light.
* **Optional Bonus:** End-to-end pipeline efficiency.
